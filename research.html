Research Focus

As a PhD student in Health Econometrics, my research focuses on the application of structural modeling, causal inference and identification issues, and microeconometrics. I have a particular interest in policy evaluation, with a passion for the theoretical aspects of econometrics and its applications in health and population economics.

Current Research Projects

  • Formal and Informal Care: Substitutes or Complements? (with Liliane Bonnal and Pascal Favard) – Investigating the relationship between formal and informal care for elderly populations with three different methods: Simultaneous Latent Factor Model (SLFM), Control Function Model (CFM), and Asymptotic Least Squares (ALS) estimator, across three pairs of care definitions.
  • Maternal Health Behaviors, Sexual and Reproductive Experiences, and Child Full Vaccination Coverage: Causal or Not? – A study using a control function and copula-free IV two-stage approach to analyze the causal relationships between these maternal behaviors and their child's vaccination status.
  • Formal Care, Informal Care, and Mental Health of the Elderly in Europe: A Structural Causal Approach (with Liliane Bonnal, Pascal Favard, and Cornel OROS) – A study using a control function and copula-free IV to answer these questions: Did care (formal or informal) have a causal impact on the mental health of the elderly? Did the deterioration of mental health impact their recourse to care (formal/informal care, domestic/personal care)?
