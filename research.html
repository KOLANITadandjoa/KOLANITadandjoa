Research Focus

As a PhD student in Health Econometrics, my research focuses on the application of structural modeling, causal inference, and microeconometrics. I am particularly interested in policy evaluation, with a passion for the theoretical aspects of econometrics and its applications in health and population economics.

Current Research Projects

  • Formal and Informal Care: Substitutes or Complements? (with Liliane Bonnal and Pascal Favard) – Investigating the relationship between formal and informal care for elderly populations using different econometric methods.
  • Causal Effects of Maternal Behaviors on Child Vaccination Coverage – A study employing control functions and copula functions to analyze the causal relationships between maternal behaviors and child vaccination status.
  • Formal Care, Informal Care, and Mental Health of the Elderly in Europe (with Liliane Bonnal, Pascal Favard, and Cornel OROS) – Evaluating the causal impact of care on the mental health of elderly populations.
