Research Focus

As a PhD student in Health Econometrics, my research focuses on the application of structural modeling, causal inference and identification issues, and microeconometrics. I have a particular interest in policy evaluation, with a passion for the theoretical aspects of econometrics and its applications in health and population economics.

Current Research Projects

I am currently working on several exciting research projects that combine innovative econometric techniques with real-world health and policy issues:

  • Formal and Informal Care: Substitutes or Complements? (with Liliane Bonnal and Pascal Favard) – Investigating the relationship between formal and informal care for elderly populations and its impact on health outcomes.
  • Maternal Health Behaviors, Sexual and Reproductive Experiences, and Full Vaccination Coverage: Causal or Not? – A study using a control function and copula-free IV two-stage approach to analyze the causal relationships between maternal health behaviors and vaccination coverage.
  • Formal Care, Informal Care, and Mental Health of the Elderly in Europe: A Structural Causal Approach – Examining the causal relationship between formal and informal care and its effects on the mental health of elderly individuals across Europe.

These projects apply cutting-edge econometric techniques to assess the impact of health policies, caregiving arrangements, and maternal health behaviors on individual and societal well-being. The aim is to better inform policy decisions that improve health outcomes, especially for vulnerable populations.
